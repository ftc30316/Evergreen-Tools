Intro

This page provides a quick overview of how we approach autonomous, tele-op, and endgame, including the timing cues and communication patterns we typically use during a match. Sharing this information up front helps reduce surprises and allows our alliance to perform more efficiently. We focus on consistent execution in autonomous, efficient cycles and awareness in tele-op, and early, clearly communicated commitments in endgame. We’re always open to adapting our role to best support the alliance and maximize overall scoring. Below we also have who is on our drive team!

Tele-Op

Cycle time

  • We prioritize fast, consistent cycles over motif patterns.
  • We are more consistent in our scoring from the lower launch zone, but can also shoot from the upper launch zone. This makes us compatible with most teams.

Drive coach communication

We work best with: a human player and active partner comms (simple, short callouts).
  • We communicate with our human player by saying "Coming in", to signal them to remove their hands from the artifacts to avoid fouls.
  • Our drive coach communicates when to slow down and shoot with our drivers.
  • We also offer motivation and cheering to all teams!

Endgame

We typically give drivers the last 20 seconds to park and secure points.

Parking plan

  • Full park: commit early if field traffic is high.
  • Partial park: if we’re finishing a final cycle, we switch at ~20s.
Alliance cue: At 0:25 we call “endgame soon”; at 0:20 we commit.
