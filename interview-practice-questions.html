FTC 30316 — Judge Q&A Practice Guide

Use these questions to practice for your 5-minute Q&A with judges and for pit visits at qualifier events. Focus on clear, confident, student-led answers.

1. Team Identity & Roles
Who you are, what you do, and how you work together.
Inspire Team Culture

Team Story & Roles

  • What makes your team unique this season?
  • How did each team member contribute to the robot, portfolio, and outreach?
  • What new skills did you personally learn this year?
  • How do you resolve disagreements on your team?
  • Tell us about a moment this year when someone stepped up as a leader.

Team Processes

  • How do you organize meetings and decide who works on what?
  • What is your engineering process from idea → prototype → test → final?
  • What tools do you use to stay organized (code repos, notes, schedules, etc.)?

2. Robot Hardware Design
Mechanisms, durability, tradeoffs, and testing.
Design Award Build Quality

Conceptual Design

  • What were your most important design goals for the robot?
  • Why did you choose your drivetrain, and what alternatives did you consider?
  • What trade-offs did you consider when designing your intake or scoring mechanism?
  • Explain how your robot achieves consistency when scoring artifacts.

Mechanisms & Materials

  • Walk us through your intake system. What problems did earlier versions have?
  • Where did you use 3D-printed parts, and why were prints better than off-the-shelf parts?
  • What design decisions improved durability of your robot?
  • What is the “weakest link” of your robot mechanically, and how do you plan to improve it?

Reliability & Testing

  • How do you test your mechanisms for repeatability?
  • What failures have you experienced, and what did you learn from them?
  • How easy is your robot to repair during a match timeout?

3. Software, Programming & Control
Code structure, sensors, autonomy, and debugging.
Control Award Autonomous

Programming Fundamentals

  • What is the most complex piece of code on your robot?
  • How do you structure your code to keep it organized and maintainable?
  • How are you using motion planning or Road Runner this season? What was hardest to tune?

Control Systems & Sensors

  • What sensors do you use on your robot and why?
  • How does sensor data improve your robot’s performance?
  • How does your robot decide when to intake and when to stop?

Autonomous

  • What is your autonomous strategy for this game?
  • How do you ensure your autonomous starts from a known pose or position?
  • What testing methods or data collection helped improve your auto?

Error Handling & Debugging

  • What is the biggest software bug you solved this season?
  • How do you test code changes safely before a competition?
  • What telemetry or logs do you rely on when debugging issues?
▶
4. Game Strategy & Match Planning
How you play the DECODE game with partners and within the rules.
Strategy Rules

Season Strategy

  • What is your overarching match strategy for this season’s game?
  • How did you decide what your robot should do versus what it could do?
  • What do you prioritize in the first 30 seconds of TeleOp?
  • What is your contingency plan if the intake breaks in a match?

Alliance Collaboration

  • How do you communicate with alliance partners before a match?
  • What do you discuss in the 5-minute pre-match strategy talk?
  • How does your robot complement other robots on a typical alliance?

Rules Knowledge

  • Which rules had the biggest impact on your robot design choices?
  • What fouls do you think are easiest to accidentally incur this year?
  • How do you avoid causing major penalties during defense or navigation?

5. Engineering Portfolio & Documentation
How you show your work, iterations, and decisions.
Portfolio Design Process

Portfolio Content

  • What would you say is the strongest section of your portfolio?
  • How does your portfolio reflect your overall design process?
  • Which diagrams or tables best show your engineering thinking?

Iteration Evidence

  • Give an example of a design that changed significantly after testing.
  • How do you document failures and what you learned from them?
  • How did your portfolio evolve from early season to now?

6. Innovation & Creativity
Unique ideas, clever solutions, and experimentation.
Innovate Award Creative Design

Innovation Decisions

  • What is the most innovative part of your robot this season?
  • What alternative solutions did you explore before choosing your current design?
  • What did you do this year that no other rookie team is likely doing?

Problem Solving

  • Tell us about a problem that required a creative, non-obvious solution.
  • How do you brainstorm as a team when you get stuck?
  • Which prototype surprised you the most, and why?

7. Team Dynamics, Communication & GP
How you treat each other, other teams, and your community.
Inspire Core Values

Team Culture

  • How do you ensure everyone has a voice on the team?
  • What does Gracious Professionalism mean to you in practice?
  • Tell us about a time you helped another team, or another team helped you.

Communication

  • How do you communicate complex engineering ideas among students with different skill levels?
  • How do drivers communicate during a match?
  • How did you divide responsibilities when preparing your presentation?

8. Outreach, Community Impact & Growth
How you share STEM and FIRST beyond your team.
Connect Award Sustainability

Outreach Impact

  • What outreach did you do this season, and why does it matter?
  • How do you measure the impact of your outreach events?
  • What audiences did you target and why?

Sustainability

  • How are you ensuring your team survives into year 2 and beyond?
  • How are you recruiting new team members or mentors?
  • What long-term partnerships are you forming with local organizations or sponsors?

9. Pit Visit Rapid-Fire Questions
Short, direct questions judges might ask in the pit.
Pit Readiness

Robot-Focused

  • Show me the part of the robot you’re most proud of.
  • What part of the robot would you redesign first if given one more week?
  • How do you maintain this mechanism between matches?

Rapid Explanations

  • Explain your drivetrain in 20 seconds.
  • Explain your autonomous strategy in 15 seconds.
  • Explain your wiring strategy and cable management choices.

10. Rookie Team-Specific Questions
How you grew in your first year of FTC.
Rookie Team Reflection

Learning & Growth

  • What was hardest about transitioning into FTC robotics?
  • What did you learn about hardware this year that surprised you?
  • What is one mistake you made early in the season that you will not repeat next year?

Preparation & Reflection

  • What does success look like for you today, as a rookie team?
  • How have you grown since your first meeting as Evergreen Dynamics?
  • What are your goals if you advance beyond this qualifier?
Tip: Practice answering out loud in complete sentences. Judges are looking for student voice, clear explanations, and honest reflection more than “perfect” answers.
